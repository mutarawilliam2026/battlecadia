# Battlecadia

The agentic era is here and `BattleCadia` is an interactive decision engine that solves choice paralysis through head-to-head bracket battles. Instead of scrolling through endless reviews or lists, users pick winners in rapid-fire 1v1 matchups across categories like movies, books, food, and products until a single champion is crowned.

Traditional shopping, movie, groceries and review sites like Yelp, Amazon, Letterboxd overwhelm users with lists, star ratings, and long-form reviews. Making a decision on those platforms feels like work. While these platforms have their advantages, `BattleCadia` replaces passive consumption with gamified micro-choices. Pitting options directly against each other forces instant clarity.

Because consumer attention spans are shrinking especially genz and gen alpha, `Battlecadia's` interactive tournament-style discovery generates significantly higher engagement and yields much cleaner preference data than standard 5-star rating systems.

I noticed how often I wasted 20 minutes just trying to pick a movie or decide what to eat because standard review platforms present too much noise and hard to navigate lists. The easiest way human brains make choices is by comparing two things at a time. I built `BattleCadia` to make decisions fast, fun, and definitive.

## The Flow: Shopping example

1. I want to buy a new pair of shoes for men size 10.5 and under $500 dollars
2. AI searches for the products and returns 10 items to compete.
3. Arenas are created based on the prompt i.e. filters like size and color. No fixed filters like current websites present.
4. A user selects an arena and the battle begins.
5. Selecting one product eliminates the other until all 10 items have battled. There are two battle times: winner stays and      knockout rounds.
6. Once a victor is chosen, the user can buy.
7. A user doesn't have to wait for the battle to end.
8. A user is then taken to the buy screen where they may choose from various vendors if any and then click buy.
